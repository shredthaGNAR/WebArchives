Then comes the time where I begin to pass the threshold of my amassed winnings and instead of stopping I try to fucking chase the few bucks that I had previously (note that at this point I am still up a considerable sum from original buy-in). You know how this ends, I go on a bad streak and then end up betting $500 bucks a hand and then go on a run but then lose some double downs and splits and before I know it I have wasted 3+ hours and now I am breaking even and though I havent technically lose money, I feel as if I do. Next comes, the chase and usually ends up with my buy in busted and me with a incredible amount of rage!

It is often said that people whom are successful at sport betting and poker (both of which I consider myself to be rather good at) have vices and mine is I love action. The problem is precipitated by the fact that I had gnarly surgery on my ankle and thus am unable to get around town and do activities I usually enjoy.

For example today (my NHL picks below) went 4-1 +5.2 units, which is a great day sports wagering and overall this year I am up over 60+ units on the NHL alone. However, I ruin my day by playing this stupid online blackjack at midnight that lasts me 1-3 hours or even 8 hours when it gets bad. I seem to get it the zone and it is so out of my element to do so, because when I make sports wagers or play poker I am very mathematical and cognizant and am able to control myself. In regards to blackjack, it sucks me in and pisses the hell out of me: why can I not control myself? I had such a great day sports betting but then i decided to play a few $5 hands which turned in to a mini-marathon in which I lost more than 5 units. Furthermore, during this session I was up $1000 dollars and that was to be my threshold in which if I went below it I would quit. But what happened is I far exceeded that number but then went on a losing streak and was at $975 and couldnt get back to the $1000 so I kept playing. How stupid is that! Lose my buy-in and my profit for $25 dollars? The worst is when you play for a long period of time, lasting until the early morning and then go broke: nothing like trying to eat breakfast after losing $5000 dollars (I was up 5 dimes, I didnt buy in for that). One time I thought it would be a good idea to bet $800 dollars a hand! Imagine doing that in a brick and mortar casino; i could never do that. Just has casinos were genius for changing peoples bills to chips; its even worse online when its virtual money.

The worst part of this is how much of a MARK I feel: a sucker. Thats exactly what they online books want you to do and I fucking fell for it. I enjoy blackjack and have no problems when Im in a casino, however something about the online casino just makes a fool of me. Its the easy access and the virtual money added with the boredom and need for action that precipitates this problem. It is just a drag because it is totally against my nature esp. in the gambling realm: i try to make rational decisions and use statistics and have a real thought-out plan- pretty much try not to be a sucker and thats what angers me the most.

Reading NHL Betting Lines and Movement for your Benefit

Jan 6th

Posted by gmckay in Advice

No comments

Its late so excuse the contrite write-up, I assure I will discuss this topic at further length tomorrow or asap, because I feel it is a great tool in which to use: especially in NHL hockey betting where a great deal of information can be derived from the line itself and its movement throughout the day or so in which it open to public betting.

The reason I bring this topic up is because of a game last night involving Nashville playing Anaheim on the the road. I had made a play earlier in the day that by the time I posted my picks for the day, the value in the line had vastly changed and this was not because of lopsided action on NSH or an injury. The resulting line was indicative of a line set by the oddsmakers that wanted ANA action and not NSH, whom they liked to win in regulation time.

Let me explain:

First of all the line was really low on the Anaheim Ducks considering they had won 3 in a row and 2 of those vs. CHI and PHI, two good teams. On the contrary, the Nashville Predators had lost 5 of 7 and their only wins were vs. MIN and CBJ, nothing to brag home about. Also, you must take into account that the Predators have a veeeerryyy little fan base/action because they are NOT a popular team whatsoever: not even in Nashville! hah.

Lets look at the line yesterday for the game: You could take ANA - a puck for +140! Compare that to what they were offering on the Preds getting a +  puck: you would have to -152! (Preds + -152) Thats insane! There is no way you can take that bet, the odd-makers obviously wanted (imo) for people to be on Ducks- look at those odds! They wanted people off the Preds and ON the Ducks. The line was one in which they wanted to entice people to take the Ducks and stay off the Preds because they had a pretty good feeling the Preds where going to win in reg. Also if you look at the movement of the line and the betting percentage, much can be inferred and used to profit. A rough approximation of betting % for the game was 37% on Preds and the rest 63% on ANA and the line experienced RLM (reverse line movement): opening at +120 and dropping all the way to +105 or at some gin joints +100(EVEN). Usually the house wants equal action and is happy with the vig, but as you can see in this case, the line that would typically move in favor of the Predators, making them +130 etc, went the opposite way and thus made it less desirable (in the typical sorts) because you are now getting less value. Most people would be like,  Why would I take Nashville at +105 when I didnt like it at +120? Perhaps I would have liked it at +130 but now the Ducks are -115: I love it, a steal! One more thing, if you look at the betting percentages of the line throughout the day or so of it being open, you will discover a large spike of Nashville money immediately when the line first opened (80% or so). Then after that the action is all on ANA. That is another useful indicator: what does the opening line betting % look like? Because who typically bets a game a day prior? Usually informed people aka sharps and they jumped on Nashville at +120.
