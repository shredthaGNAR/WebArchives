West Seneca Police Department

August 17, 2021 
West Seneca Police Blotter Report
8/8/21 to 8/14/21
The following is a sample of calls handled by patrol.
Sunday, August 8th
- A physical altercation between a male and female was reported in the parking lot of the Bluebird Motel on Southwestern Blvd. Patrol made contact with the female who stated she had an altercation with another female but did not require any police assistance at this time.
- Shortly after midnight a Boncroft Dr resident reported that a suspicious male knocked on their front window and got in a vehicle that drove away. Patrol stopped that vehicle who stated they were having a late night meeting with a friend and were on the wrong part of the street.
- A dog was reported to left in a van in a parking lot of a Southwestern Blvd apartment complex. Patrol responded however there was no dog in the vehicle.
- A Dwyer St resident reported a neighbor sprayed chemicals in their garden and killed the plants as part of an ongoing neighbor dispute. Patrol reported some dead weeds in the garden that likely died of natural causes. No evidence was present indicating chemicals were used.
- The manager of a retailer on Union Rd called for assistance with an employee who customers were complaining was acting erratic and may be under the influence of narcotics. The subject was relieved of his duties by the manager and transported home by patrol.
- A yellow Camaro was reported to be driving down Seneca St blowing an airhorn out the window.
- A Tampa Drive resident reported they heard a person in their basement. Patrol checked the residence and no one else was inside.
- 19 traffic cones being used to block off traffic for parking on Indian Church Rd during the carnival were stolen. Detectives are investigating.
- At around 10:30pm, a Seneca Creek Rd resident reported their 49 year old neighbor was caught running around naked outside of their house. When confronted he fled in his vehicle. The complainant was advised on the process to pursue charges against the man if she should choose to do so.
- A Dirkson Ave resident reported a neighbor was making excessive noise taking apart a washing machine in his backyard. All was quiet upon patrol's arrival.
Monday, August 9th
- Two males inside 7-Eleven on Southwestern were reported to be harassing other customers. Patrol spoke with the store clerk who stated they did not have any issues and the call was made by a third party. The individuals had left.
- A 46 year old Cheektowaga man was pulled over for speeding and crossing over lanes of traffic on Union Rd. When he was stopped, the man told the officer "you are making me late for work where he drives a waste disposal truck. Patrol noticed the man had slurred speech, bloodshot eyes, and a strong odor of alcohol coming from his breath. He failed several field sobriety tests and was placed under arrest. He was returned to the station where he submitted to a breath test which resulted in a BAC of .29%. He was charged with Aggravated DWI and several traffic violations and released to a sober relative. The vehicle was impounded.
- Several calls were received for an altercation at a Harlem Rd apartment complex between a male and a female. It was reported that a 33 year old Cheektowaga man had struck the mother of his child several times while she was holding their infant child. The man continued to strike the woman and then forcibly pulled her hair, took her iPhone, and then smashed it on the ground. The male party fled upon witnesses calling 911. Witnesses provided video evidence of the attack and patrol filed charges on the victims behalf. The following day, the subject arrived to the West Seneca Police station to turn himself in on an unrelated traffic warrant. He was subsequently taken into custody and charged with Robbery in the 3rd degree- a D Felony, Grand Larceny 4th- an E Felony, Criminal Mischief 3rd- an E Felony, Harrasment 2nd, and Endangering the Welfare of a Child. He was arraigned in WS Justice Court and released on his own recognizance and issued an Order of Protection. The incident was also referred to Child Protection.
- A male and female were reported to be looking into windows and vehicles at the Mill Rd school building. Patrol located several youths who were playing a game of hide and seek.
Tuesday, August 10th
- A 19 year old male was located on Fieldcrest Ct staggering and throwing up in the roadway. He was driven home and placed in the care of his grandmother.
- A South Fisher Rd resident called to report he had hired an escort to come over to his residence, and that after they completed their transaction, he fell asleep. The man reported the woman then took his car keys and stole his vehicle. He could not provide any additional information or a name of the suspect. The man called dispatch later that day to report he was able to recover his vehicle and did not wish to pursue the matter any further.
- Patrol responded to a report of a vehicle in a ditch on Indian Church Rd. Officers located a 34 year old West Seneca man walking out of a nearby building stating a friend was driving the vehicle. When questioned further, the man admitted to driving and panicked because he just got on parole. The mans license was found to be revoked for a prior DWAI. The man also appeared to be under the influence. He was given several field sobriety tests which he failed. He was returned to the station where he was evaluated by a Drug Recognition Expert that concluded he was impaired by a stimulant and a depressant. He refused a blood draw, and was charged with DWI-Drugs, Aggravated Unlicensed Operation 1st, leaving the scene of a property damage accident, unlicensed operation, and a moving violation. He was released to the care of a relative. The vehicle was impounded.
- A vehicle was reported to be operating recklessly on Union and East & West Rds. Patrol made contact with the driver who stated he reached for a tool bag which slid in the vehicle and may have swerved.
- A resident reported cash may have been taken from her purse during the Town Board Meeting. She stated she will follow up with the town to look into the possibility of any video evidence.
- Numerous residents in neighborhoods in the area of Seneca Creek Rd reported their mailboxes were opened and mail was scattered in the roadway. Patrol collected the mail and returned it to the correct addresses.
Wednesday, August 11th
- Several road obstructions and multiple tree limbs were reported down in roadways and on power lines throughout town.
- Seneca Hose and Winchester Fire responded to the NYS Thruway for an accident between a passenger vehicle and a tractor trailer with extrication. One person was transported to the hospital. The accident was handled by NYSP.
- A Burch Ave resident reported they were being threatened by their neighbor and his dog. Patrol responded and reported both parties were insulting each other and that the dog was friendly. They were advised to remain civil with each other.
- Winchester Fire responded to Harlem Rd Park for a report of an 11 year old stuck in a tree. The child was brought down from the tree safely.
- A dog was reportedly left unattended in a vehicle in the parking lot of PetCo. Patrol responded and found the vehicle was running and the dog was not in any distress.
- A patron at Holidays on Harlem Rd reportedly came into the bar intoxicated. After being served one drink, she began harassing and swearing at other patrons and was cut off by the bartender. The subject, a 61 year old West Seneca woman, sat quietly at the bar for a few minutes, then completely unprovoked, she threw a beer bottle at the back of the head of the bartender. The bottle narrowly missed the bartender and struck numerous liquor bottles on the bar, causing them to fall and break. The woman then left the bar. Patrol located the woman a short time later at her residence on Knox Ave. She became belligerent and combative with patrol and was taken into custody. She was issued an appearance ticket for Attempted Assault 3rd, Criminal Mischief 4th, and Criminal Possession of a Weapon 4th. She was then transported to ECMC for a mental health evaluation.
- Patrol received a call for a suspicious male on the property of a Lakeside Dr residence where he is not allowed. Upon arrival, patrol located the 27 year old West Seneca resident who has an active stay away order of protection from the residence. He was taken into custody without incident. Investigation revealed the subject had also stolen an Amazon package from the porch of the homeowner. He was transported to WSPD, charged with Criminal Contempt 2nd, Criminal Possession of Stolen Property 5th, and Petit Larceny. He was arraigned the following morning by WS Justice Court and remanded to the Erie County Holding Center in lieu of $5,000 cash bail/ $10,000 bond.
- Several garbage cans on Borden Rd were knocked over by unknown youths.
Thursday, August 12th
- A large fight involving approximately 15 people was reported at the SC Lounge on Clinton St at around 12:15 am. While headed to the call, it was additionally reported that one of the patrons had displayed a handgun. Upon arrival, a white SUV was seen fleeing the scene, and while doing so, struck a bar patron that was standing on Electric Ave, knocking him to the ground. He suffered minor injuries. The vehicle then fled from pursuing patrol cars and entered the I190 North towards downtown. Patrol pursued the vehicle off of the 190 to Hamburg St where the vehicle continued to William St where it was stopped with the assistance of Buffalo Police on Wilson St. The driver, a 30 year old Buffalo resident, was taken into custody and returned to the bar for a positive show-up identification and was also identified on bar surveillance cameras. No weapons were found on the subject or in the vehicle. The man showed numerous signs of intoxication and refused all sobriety tests and refused to cooperate with the booking process. He was charged with Menacing in the 2nd degree, Reckless Endangerment 2nd, DWI, Unlawful Fleeing of a Police Officer, Reckless Driving, Leaving the Scene of an Injury Accident, and 22 other vehicle and traffic law tickets and moving violations. He was released on an appearance ticket in accordance with NYS Bail regulations and transported to ECMC for treatment of an eye injury suffered during the bar altercation.
- A North Ave resident reported his vehicle that was stolen the week prior and recovered in Buffalo contained a Glock handgun that he just realized was missing. He is following up with Buffalo Police.
- Dollar General on Seneca St reported a male ran out of the store with two baskets of merchandise. Detectives are investigating.
Friday, August 13th
- At around 3am patrol located a stolen Hyundai Sonata out of the City of Tonawanda operating on Center Rd. Officers pulled the vehicle over and detained the vehicle occupants. The driver, a 44 year old Buffalo man, stated he borrowed the vehicle from a friend. He also had 8 active license suspensions/revocations for DWI and failure to pay child support. He also had an active stay away order of protection from the passenger, a 30 year old Buffalo woman. Recovered from the vehicle during inventory were baggies of suspected methamphetamine and a crack cocaine pipe. Both individuals were returned to the station for processing. The male was charged with 2 counts of Criminal Possession of a Controlled Substance 7th, Criminal Contempt 2nd, Unauthorized Use of a Motor Vehicle 3rd, 2 counts of Aggravated Unlicensed Operation 2nd and Unlicensed Operation. He was released on an appearance ticket to return to court at a later date. The female was also charged with 2 counts of Criminal Possession of a Controlled Substance 7th and Unauthorized Use of a Motor Vehicle 3rd. She was released on an appearance ticket and turned over to Buffalo Police on an outstanding arrest warrant.
- A Reserve Rd resident reported she was scammed out of $2500 in gift cards through Facebook Marketplace while attempting to purchase a vehicle.
- A vehicle windshield was broken on Legion Drive after it was struck by a baseball.
- Wegmans reported a male was attempting to steal steaks and seafood. He fled the store and dropped the product when confronted. Patrol impounded the suspects bicycle that he left behind.
- A van was reported to have crashed into multiple parked cars on Lyndale Ct. Patrol responded and located the driver, a 61 year old West Seneca resident, who was sitting in the vehicle while it was still running. He stated he had several beers at the Bisons game, showed signs of intoxication, and failed several field sobriety tests. He was returned to the station where he failed a breath test. He was released on an appearance ticket for DWI and moving from lane unsafely and released to a sober adult.
- A patron at Gordys Too on Seneca St was reported to be unruly at the bar. He was escorted off the premises and walked home without incident.
Saturday, August 14th
- An intoxicated female reportedly wandered away from a wedding reception at Klocs Grove on Seneca Creek Rd in the rain and without her cell phone. She was missing for approximately 90 minutes before she contacted a family member over the phone to report she made it safely back to her hotel.
- A woman reported she lost her credit card at the Dollar Tree in Southgate Plaza, and someone used the card shortly after at that store and then at McDonalds. A brief time later two individuals, who reside at a nearby NYS group home, approached the woman and returned her card and apologized for taking it. The victim did not wish to pursue any criminal charges.
- A Tudor Bd resident reported his house was egged again which has been an ongoing issue.
- A vehicle was reportedly stolen from 7-Eleven on French Rd in Cheektowaga and was GPS tracked to the Tops on Harlem Rd. The vehicle was located unoccupied by patrol and Cheektowaga PD in the parking lot. Officers located a suspect fitting the description coming out of the store. He was detained and turned over to Cheektowaga PD. Their investigation is ongoing.
- A male riding a bicycle was traveling southbound on the shoulder in the northbound lane of Union Rd in front of Tim Hortons. A vehicle exited the drive-thru in front of the bicycle, causing the bicyclist to strike the side of the vehicle. The vehicle drove away and then parked on Summit Ave. When the bicyclist confronted the driver, the man kicked his bicycle rim, denting it. The man then drove away. Officers from the Accident Investigation Unit are investigating.
- An altercation was reported in the parking lot in front of Applebees on Union Rd. A female was reported to have fallen on the ground after intervening in an altercation between two males. Patrol located the individuals who were all highly intoxicated and having an argument over their favorite songs from the Billy Joel concert they had just left.
